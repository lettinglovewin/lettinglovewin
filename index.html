Letting Love Win by Mohammed Fyaz

The compelling story of a British Muslim who fought against abuse, racism and disillusionment to find God's unconditional love. Rejected by his family as an apostate, Fyaz highlights some of the cultural issues facing converts today. Fyaz grew up as a British-born Pakistani in Northern England. His troubled childhood was full of abuse, racism, and a growing disillusionment with Islam. It eventually led him to rock bottom and criminal behaviour. God, however, was purusing Fyaz. Slowly but surely He guided Fyaz to Him largely through the kindness of strangers. When Fyaz finally gave his life to God his family completely rejected him as an apostate, the consequences of which he still lives with today. While still hoping for reconciliation, Fyaz shares his heart for the calling he believes God has given him to help reach the Muslim community with the message that God's love can indeed win. Review by Lord Carey, former Archbishop of Canterbury

“This is an inspiring story! Fyaz gives an utterly compelling account of his journey from Islam to Christianity. It is a tragedy that what should be an entirely free and uncontroversial choice has resulted in the loss of his family and exclusion from the Pakistani community. To be an ‘apostate’ is to be shunned, persecuted and even violently attacked. Fyaz highlights the difficulties that face those who convert but, in spite of this, he bravely demonstrates much practical love and care for the Muslim community. In particular, he calls upon the church to evangelise sensitively and lovingly among Muslims in Britain."

A Long Walk To 10 Downing Street

Mohammed Fyaz will walk with two Pakistani-born friends approximately 300 miles in 25 days while carrying a 12 foot cross on wheels with a petition for the Prime Minister. His destination is the Prime Minister's residence at 10 Downing Street in London where a small delegation from Parliament will be waiting for him to deliver the petition.

We ask you to help support this valiant effort to draw attention to the persecution faced by followers of Jesus around the world. Your donations will help to establish a specialized safe house for ex-Muslims believers in Jesus who are in danger in the UK.

We will inform you of the details of the walk once a date has been finalized.

My Human Right to Leave Islam

I call upon the UK government to review existing police and local authority policies, procedures and officers' training in regards to victims of honour violence against ex-Muslims, and to help Dostana Ministries set up a hotline to record data on hate crimes against former Muslims by Muslims. It is time for the government to take action to protect the religious freedom of those who want to leave Islam. Ex-Muslims like Nissar Hussain should not have to go into hiding in the UK.

If we are to remain a free society it is vital that everyone should be able to choose their faith, or no faith. Threats of death for those who leave Islam must be dealt with by Government and the Police.
